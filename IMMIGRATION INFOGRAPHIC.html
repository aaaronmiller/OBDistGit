> A Decade of Deportations (FY 2011-2020)

This chart illustrates the total number of formal removals conducted by ICE over a ten-year period. It shows that removal numbers peaked in the early 2010s and were on a downward trend before the Trump administration. The totals during FY 2017-2019 were lower than any year between FY 2011 and FY 2014.

> FY 2020: Removals vs. Expulsions

The sharp drop in formal removals in FY 2020 was largely due to the implementation of Title 42, a public health order allowing for rapid expulsions at the border. These expulsions are not legally classified as removals and created a significant statistical shift.

> Workforce: Ambition vs. Reality

A 2017 executive order mandated hiring 10,000 new ICE agents. However, logistical and bureaucratic hurdles meant the actual increase was far smaller, highlighting a significant gap between the political goal and the operational reality.

> ICE Workforce Composition

Of ICE's ~21,000 employees, only a fraction are deportation officers directly responsible for removals. The majority consists of Homeland Security Investigations (HSI) agents, legal staff, and other support personnel.

> ICE Budget Growth (FY 2018-2024)

Contrary to any notion of cuts, ICE's budget has seen consistent and significant increases. Congressional appropriations have steadily grown, with funds primarily allocated to expand detention capacity and enforcement operations.

> The Policy-to-Outcome Pipeline

The data reveals a recurring theme: aggressive policy goals and increased funding did not automatically lead to record-breaking results. Real-world operational constraints consistently tempered the final outcomes.
