Kerr County, Texas Flood - July 4, 2025

Overview

Torrential rains triggered catastrophic flash flooding in Kerr County, Texas, along the Guadalupe River, with the river surging 26 feet in 45 minutes. Up to 10–15 inches of rain fell overnight, causing at least 24 deaths and leaving 23–25 girls missing from Camp Mystic.

[](https://www.usatoday.com/story/news/weather/2025/07/04/guadalupe-river-flooding-deaths-texas-kerrville/84470317007/)[](https://www.cnn.com/weather/live-news/texas-flooding-07-04-2025)

Flood Duration and Recession

  • Peak Height: The Guadalupe River surged from 7 to over 29 feet in less than 2 hours, peaking around 4:35 a.m. on July 4, 2025. The exact duration at peak is unclear due to gauge failure, but likely brief.
    • [](https://www.newsweek.com/central-texas-flooding-live-tracker-maps-2094900)
  • Recession: Waters began receding in some areas within an hour (e.g., Camp Mystic), but remained high into July 5, with the river near Spring Branch cresting at 37.2 feet at 2 a.m. Complete recession likely took hours to days.
    • [](https://www.cnn.com/weather/live-news/texas-flooding-07-04-2025)

Affected Area

  • Geographic Scope: Flooding centered in Kerr County (1,107 sq miles), impacting Hunt, Kerrville, Ingram, and Center Point along the Guadalupe River. Neighboring Kendall and Tom Green counties also affected.
    • [](https://www.usatoday.com/story/news/weather/2025/07/04/guadalupe-river-flooding-deaths-texas-kerrville/84470317007/)[](https://www.newsweek.com/central-texas-flooding-live-tracker-maps-2094900)
  • Flood Corridor: Affected a 20–30-mile river corridor from Hunt to downstream communities. Low-lying areas, camps, and neighborhoods like Bumble Bee Hills were hardest hit.
    • [](https://www.tpr.org/2025-07-04/kerr-county-residents-urged-not-to-travel-unless-seeking-higher-ground-along-guadalupe-as-severe-flooding-leads-to-fatalities)

Population Impacted

  • Kerr County Population: ~52,598 (2020 Census). Thousands affected due to evacuations, property damage, and infrastructure disruption.
    • [](https://www.nytimes.com/live/2025/07/04/us/texas-floods-guadalupe-river)
  • Casualties: At least 24 deaths, with 23–25 girls missing from Camp Mystic (750 campers). 237 people rescued, 167 by helicopter.
    • [](https://www.cnn.com/weather/live-news/texas-flooding-07-04-2025)[](https://www.foxnews.com/us/devastating-texas-river-flood-leaves-least-6-dead-emergency-crews-race-find-others-missing)

Historical Comparison

  • Last Major Event: The 1987 Guadalupe River flood (11 inches of rain, 10 deaths) was the closest comparable event, 38 years prior. The 2025 flood surpassed it in intensity.
    • [](https://www.washingtonpost.com/weather/2025/07/04/texas-flooding-extreme-rains/)[](https://www.ksat.com/news/local/2025/07/04/fatalities-confirmed-in-kerr-county-flooding-along-guadalupe-river-sheriffs-office-says/)
  • Unprecedented Speed: The 2025 event’s 26-foot rise in 45 minutes was faster than 1987, making it a rare catastrophe.

Likelihood of Recurrence

  • Risk Factors: Texas Hill Country’s hilly terrain and dry soils increase flash flood risk. The 2025 event was a 1-in-100-year flood, but climate change may raise future likelihood.
    • [](https://www.cnn.com/2025/07/04/weather/texas-flooding-climate)
  • Future Outlook: More rain was forecast through July 5, with ongoing risks from San Antonio to Waco. Improved forecasting and infrastructure could mitigate future events.
    • [](https://www.reuters.com/business/environment/officials-confirm-deaths-after-flash-floods-central-texas-2025-07-04/)

Why It Wasn’t Dealt with Properly

  • Underestimated Forecasts: NWS predicted 1–5 inches, but 10–15 inches fell, overwhelming preparations.
    • [](https://www.usatoday.com/story/news/weather/2025/07/04/guadalupe-river-flooding-deaths-texas-kerrville/84470317007/)[](https://www.reuters.com/business/environment/officials-confirm-deaths-after-flash-floods-central-texas-2025-07-04/)
  • Rapid Onset: Flooding began pre-dawn, with a 26-foot surge in 45 minutes, limiting evacuation time.
    • [](https://www.newsweek.com/texas-flooding-harris-kerr-guadalupe-river-weather-warning-2094777)
  • Infrastructure Issues: The USGS gauge failed at 4:35 a.m., and dry soils increased runoff.
    • [](https://www.tpr.org/2025-07-04/kerr-county-residents-urged-not-to-travel-unless-seeking-higher-ground-along-guadalupe-as-severe-flooding-leads-to-fatalities)[](https://www.cnn.com/2025/07/04/weather/texas-flooding-climate)

Why It Was a Surprise

  • Unpredictable Scale: The rapid 26-foot rise and 10–15 inches of rain exceeded forecasts, driven by Tropical Storm Barry’s remnants.
    • [](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/July_2025_Central_Texas_floods)
  • Holiday Timing: The July 4 holiday increased visitors, complicating response.
    • [](https://www.newsweek.com/central-texas-flooding-live-tracker-maps-2094900)
  • Comparison to 1987: The 2025 flood’s speed outpaced the 1987 event, catching authorities off guard.
    • [](https://www.kens5.com/article/weather/weather-floods-kerrville-july-4-texas-evacuations/273-361090c5-4352-4e2d-84ba-3adcf2007354)

Location

  • Kerr County: 65 miles northwest of San Antonio, 90 miles west of Austin, in Texas Hill Country. Not near Houston or Dallas.
    • [](https://www.usatoday.com/story/news/weather/2025/07/04/guadalupe-river-flooding-deaths-texas-kerrville/84470317007/)
  • Guadalupe River: Flooding originated at the river’s headwaters near Hunt, flowing through Kerrville to Comfort.
    • [](https://www.tpr.org/2025-07-04/kerr-county-residents-urged-not-to-travel-unless-seeking-higher-ground-along-guadalupe-as-severe-flooding-leads-to-fatalities)
  • Canyons: Likely refers to river tributaries (e.g., North/South Forks), which funneled water into the main river.
    • [](https://www.cbsnews.com/news/texas-hill-country-severe-floods/)

Flood Sirens and Warnings

  • Sirens Activated: Sounded at 11 a.m. in Comfort, hours after flooding began at 4–5 a.m., too late for many.
    • [](https://www.usatoday.com/story/news/weather/2025/07/04/guadalupe-river-flooding-deaths-texas-kerrville/84470317007/)
  • Warning Issues: NWS issued a flood watch on July 3, but the 5:34 a.m. emergency alert came after flooding started. No robust early warning system existed.
    • [](https://www.cbsnews.com/news/texas-hill-country-severe-floods/)
  • Regional Context: Rural Kerr County lacks the dense warning systems of urban areas like Houston.
    • [](https://www.ksat.com/news/local/2025/07/04/fatalities-confirmed-in-kerr-county-flooding-along-guadalupe-river-sheriffs-office-says/)
