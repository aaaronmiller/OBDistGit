Executive Summary

This comprehensive methodology addresses the critical challenge of converting legacy scanned P&IDs into accurate, standards-compliant digital formats while enabling the AI-powered creation of new, optimized designs for refinery projects. The approach encompasses four fundamental pillars:

  • Advanced Digitization: Leveraging state-of-the-art OCR and symbol recognition technologies with 90.5% precision in pipeline identification
  • AI-Powered Design: Intelligent component selection and placement through parametric modeling and generative design capabilities
  • Standards Compliance: Embedded regulatory adherence to API, ANSI, ISA, and OSHA standards through automated validation
  • Commercial Viability: Professional review frameworks and human-in-the-loop systems ensuring engineering integrity

The methodology transforms P&IDs from static documentation into dynamic, data-driven engineering tools, enabling significant improvements in design efficiency, safety compliance, and operational optimization for high-consequence refinery facilities.

1. Digitization and Structuring of Legacy Scanned P&ID PDFs

1.1 Advanced OCR and Symbol Recognition

The initial phase leverages advanced Optical Character Recognition (OCR) and symbol recognition technologies to interpret complex visual elements in legacy P&IDs. Standard OCR solutions often fail due to the "messy" nature of P&IDs, with overlapping lines, varied fonts, and inconsistent tag placements.

Deep learning models, particularly YOLO variants, demonstrate significant promise. YOLOv8 achieved 90.5% average precision and 88.5% recall for pipeline identification when combined with split coordinate spatial attention and data augmentation.

1.2 Conversion to Structured Digital Formats

Once basic entities are extracted, the next crucial step is converting raw data into structured digital formats suitable for reports, databases, and AI applications. Graph-based representations, particularly Labeled Property Graphs (LPGs), are highly suitable for capturing both topological relationships and domain-specific semantics.

The DEXPI (Data Exchange in the Process Industry) standard provides a standardized, machine-readable format, with version 1.3 integrating ISO 15926 for data models and ISO 10628 for graphical symbols.

Component Attributes Include:

  • • Temperature limits and cost data
  • • Supplier information and standards compliance

2.3 Integration of Process Simulation for Design Validation

A cornerstone of creating reliable P&IDs is integrating process simulation directly within the AI-driven design workflow. AI-driven P&IDs linked with simulation software can validate performance, optimize control logic, and automate design updates based on simulation results.

The Andritz case study demonstrated benefits of coupling P&ID data with dynamic process simulation to investigate designs, identify issues, and test control strategies using a "Virtual Process Plant."

3. Standards Compliance and Regulatory Adherence

3.1 Embedding Industry Standards into AI Models

A critical requirement is strict adherence to all relevant regulatory and industry standards. IPS notes their AI can be "fully customized based on customer's engineering standards" and can "unify multiple standards into a common standard".

Key Standards Integration

  • • API Standards: API 610 for pumps
  • • ANSI/ISA: ISA 5.1 for instrumentation symbols
  • • OSHA: Safety and material handling requirements
  • • ISO: 15926, 10628, IEC 62424 integration

DEXPI Standard Integration

The DEXPI standard plays a pivotal role by integrating various international standards. The DEXPI-2-graph converter extracts EquipmentClass and ComponentClass information directly from DEXPI files, ensuring AI models work with standardized components.
