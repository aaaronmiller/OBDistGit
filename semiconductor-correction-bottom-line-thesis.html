I. Historical Correction Patterns — What SOX Tells Us

Critical Context: The SOX index had just completed its longest winning streak in 32 years, rising 44% since March 31 and nearly 50% in four weeks. It now accounts for a record 23% of the S&P 500. This is the most extended semiconductor rally in history — and history says corrections after such streaks are multi-week affairs, not single-day events.

Historical Precedent — 1999 Parallel: The only year with 3+ clusters of 3%+ SOX drops from new highs was 1999, which preceded the dot-com crash. However, the current AI demand thesis is fundamentally different from 1999's speculative internet bubble — AI infrastructure spending is real, contracted, and accelerating. The 1999 parallel is a risk scenario, not the base case.
Historical Precedent — 2025 Recovery: NVDA dropped 8.5% in February 2025 after a historic earnings beat, erasing $273B in value. It recovered to new highs within 6 weeks. Every NVDA correction since April 2025 has found buyers. The pattern: sharp drop → 2-4 week consolidation → recovery to new highs.

II. The Catalyst Timeline — What Drives the Next 4 Weeks

May 15 (DONE) — Macro Shock Wave 1

30-year yield hits 5.1%, oil to $104, Trump-Xi no deal, $1 trillion erased. SOX closes -3% after -6.8% intraday. This was the mechanical/technical wave — profit-taking, options expiry, and macro fear.

May 19 (Monday) — NVDA Earnings Pre-Announcement Window

Options positioning and pre-earnings anxiety typically create 2-3 days of elevated volatility before NVDA reports. Consensus expects $78.1B revenue. Goldman Sachs forecasts a $2B beat. If guidance disappoints, expect another 5-8% drop in NVDA, dragging SOX down further.

Risk: NVDA has "sold out for 2026" — but the market may already be pricing in perfection. Any hint of demand normalization = sharp sell-off.
May 20 (Tuesday) — NVDA Q1 FY2027 Earnings

The single biggest catalyst for the entire semiconductor sector. A beat + strong guidance = bottom confirmed, rally begins. A miss or cautious guidance = Wave 2 intensifies, SOX drops another 3-5%.

Key Insight: The stock has already pulled back ~8% from its peak into earnings. This is the "buy the rumor, sell the news" setup — but if guidance is strong, the post-earnings rally could be sharp (10-15%).
May 21 (Thursday) — Samsung Strike Begins

18-day union strike begins at Samsung memory fabs. 50,000 workers. Estimated cost: $700M-$20B depending on duration. Global memory production could drop 3-5%. This is a double-edged sword:

Bearish for Samsung: Production disruption, revenue loss, labor unrest signals deeper issues. Samsung already -8.61% on May 15.
Bullish for MU/TSM: Less competition = pricing power for Micron. Memory prices already up 20% on strike fears. Micron is the primary beneficiary.
May 21-31 — Samsung Strike Escalation Window

If the strike proceeds as planned, memory supply tightens further. DRAM/NAND prices could spike 5-10% within 2 weeks. This creates a self-reinforcing cycle: higher memory prices → better MU/TSM earnings → stock prices recover. But if the strike is longer than 18 days, it could disrupt the entire AI supply chain (HBM for GPUs).

Late May — Treasury Yield Inflection

The 30-year yield at 5.1% is a critical threshold. If it breaks above 5.25%, expect another leg down in tech/growth. If it stabilizes or retreats below 4.8%, the correction is likely over. Watch CPI data and Fed Chair Warsh's comments.

Early June — Bottom Formation Window

If NVDA earnings beat (May 20) and the Samsung strike is contained (May 21-31), the bottom should form in the first two weeks of June. Historical patterns suggest SOX finds its bottom 2-3 weeks after the initial shock, with a "double bottom" or "retest" pattern common.

The biggest risks to the base case are: (1) NVDA earnings miss (15% probability but high impact), (2) 30-year yield breaking 5.25% (25% probability), and (3) Samsung strike extending beyond 30 days (20% probability). Any of these could extend the correction by 2-4 weeks and deepen the drawdown to 15-25%.
